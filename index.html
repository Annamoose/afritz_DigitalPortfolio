The Grand Tour

Tour map of fictitious international bicycle race from Grand Forks, North Dakota to Grand Forks, British Columbia. Cartographic style inspired by the Grand Tour maps of Europe from the 17th to mid-18th century. Created using ArcGIS Desktop, Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.

A map is worth a thousand words

Beginning in 2011, the world's attention was turned to North Dakota as the price of oil crept upward, hundreds of new wells were drilled and high paying jobs lured people north. A three-part article in the NY Times in 2014 described North Dakotans as being unprepared for the boom. In particular, they claimed there was "slender" regulatory oversight of the industry. But how slender is slender? Examine the infographic to learn more. Created using Adobe Illustrator, ArcMap desktop and Excel.

Spatial Database Design and Analysis

Created as the final project for UW-Madison's GEOG 574 with classmates Dan Lissick and Andrew Pittman, the ND Medical Marijuana geospatial database is a potential tool for a regulatory authority or grow facility to examine the best possible placement of a medical marijuana dispensary or grow facility. Database was designed to optimize locations given required environmental, regulatory and waste disposal conditions for North Dakota communities. The database was tested using data for Fargo, North Dakota. Created using ERDPlus, PostgreSQL, PostGIS and ArcGIS Pro

HREP Explorer

Habitat Restoration and Enhancement Project (HREP) Explorerer was developed with Amanda Ross for our final project in UW-Madison's GEOG 575. The web application shows HREP projects and status in the Upper Mississippi River. The site was created using Esri Leaflet, Calcite-Bootstrap and JQuery.

Cancer Clusters & Nitrate

Interface to allows users to enter a value for K to explore relationship between nitrate concentration in groundwater to rates of cancer occurence in Wisconsin. Built using ArcGIS Desktop 10.7.1, model builder, python, and TKinter. Read the full report for details.

Sertoma Park Explorer

Users of Sertoma Park Explorer can discover park features, including accessibility of park restrooms, picnic shelters, playgrounds, and surface type of trails and play areas. Created using ArcGIS Desktop, Leaflet, PostGIS and CartoDB, the UI/UX design focused on accessibility and creating a user-submission report. Click here to read the project report.
