Maps to communicate

Beginning in 2011, the world's attention was turned to North Dakota as the price of oil crept upward, hundreds of new wells were drilled and high paying jobs lured people north. A three-part article in the NYTimes from 2014 described North Dakotans as being unprepared for the boom. In particular, they claimed there was 'slender' regulatory oversight of the industry. But how slender is slender? Examine the infographic to learn more. Created using Adobe Illustrator, ArcMap desktop and Excel.

Database Design

Along with GEOG574 classmates Dan Lissick and Andrew Pittman, used ERDPlus, PostGreSQL, and ArcGIS Pro designed a geospatial database that allows a potential regulatory authority, local planning commission or potential applicant for a medical marijuana facility to examine the best possible placement of a dispensary or grow facility given required environmental, regulatory or zoning conditions. As the largest city in North Dakota, the database was tested using data from Fargo, North Dakota. The database could be a model for site optimization for other population centers throughout North Dakota, as they try to find acceptable locations for medical marijuana growing and dispensary facilities.
